What’s the Deal?: Hiring a Personal Injury Attorney – What to Expect

Published June 2025

If you've been injured due to someone else’s negligence, you may be considering hiring a personal injury attorney. Understanding what to expect during this process is crucial for achieving the best possible outcome for your case. This guide will provide insights into the steps of hiring an attorney, the costs involved, and what you can anticipate during your legal journey.

Hiring a Personal Injury Attorney – What to Expect

Introduction

Initial Consultation

The first step in hiring a personal injury attorney is scheduling an initial consultation. Most attorneys offer this service for free. During this meeting, you'll discuss the details of your case, including the circumstances surrounding your injury and the extent of your damages.

Be prepared to share any relevant documentation, such as medical records, accident reports, and insurance correspondence. The attorney will evaluate these materials and advise you on the potential strengths and weaknesses of your case.

Understanding Fees

Cost is a significant consideration when hiring a personal injury attorney. Most personal injury attorneys work on a contingency fee basis, meaning they only get paid if you win your case. Typically, they will take around 30% to 40% of the settlement amount.

During the initial consultation, clarify how the fee structure works, including any additional costs you might incur, like court filing fees or expert witness fees. Understanding the financial aspects upfront will help you avoid surprises later on.

The Legal Process

The legal process in a personal injury case can be extensive. Here’s a brief overview of what to expect:

  • Investigation: Your attorney will conduct a thorough investigation, gathering evidence and documentation to support your claim.
  • Negotiation: Most personal injury cases are settled out of court. Your attorney will negotiate with the insurance company on your behalf to seek a fair settlement.
  • Litigation: If a satisfactory settlement cannot be reached, your attorney may file a lawsuit and represent you in court. This stage involves legal formalities, including pleadings, discovery, and potentially a trial.

Communication & Updates

Effective communication is key to a successful attorney-client relationship. Your attorney should keep you informed about developments in your case and respond to any questions you have promptly. Make sure you discuss your expectations regarding communication from the outset.

Conclusion

Hiring a personal injury attorney can significantly impact the outcome of your case. By understanding what to expect— from the initial consultation and fee structure to the entire legal process— you’ll be better prepared to make informed decisions. Remember to choose an attorney you feel comfortable with and who demonstrates a genuine commitment to your case.
