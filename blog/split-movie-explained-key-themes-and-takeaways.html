What’s the Deal?: “Split” Movie Explained – Key Themes and Takeaways

Published June 2025

The 2016 psychological thriller Split, directed by M. Night Shyamalan, has garnered a lot of attention for its compelling narrative and multi-faceted characters. As a sequel to Shyamalan’s Unbreakable, the film explores themes of identity, trauma, and the complexities of the human psyche. In this blog post, we’ll delve into the key themes and takeaways from Split, providing insights into its deeper meanings.

“Split” Movie Explained – Key Themes and Takeaways

The Power of Identity

One of the most prominent themes in Split is the exploration of identity. The film features Kevin Wendell Crumb, portrayed masterfully by James McAvoy, who suffers from Dissociative Identity Disorder (DID). Kevin hosts 23 distinct identities, each with its own unique characteristics. This distinctive portrayal invites viewers to consider the intricate nature of self and how our identities can be fragmented due to trauma. Shyamalan challenges the audience to understand not just Kevin's identities but the concept of identity as a whole, prompting questions about how we perceive ourselves and others.

Trauma and Its Effects

Trauma is another critical theme in Split. Throughout the film, we learn that Kevin's identities are a direct response to the abuse he suffered during his childhood. Each persona represents a coping mechanism that Kevin has developed to withstand his past pain. This depiction of trauma serves as a stark reminder of its long-lasting effects and how it shapes individuals in complex ways. By bringing this subject to the forefront, Shyamalan offers a thought-provoking commentary on mental health and the importance of compassion and understanding for those who have experienced trauma.

The Beast: A Metaphor for Transformation

The film’s climax introduces 'The Beast,' the most powerful identity within Kevin that embodies strength and primal instincts. This transformation can be interpreted as a metaphorical representation of how individuals can develop incredible strengths as a result of facing their inner demons. The Beast’s nature raises queries about the duality of human nature—how one can possess both greatness and darkness. It challenges the idea of good versus evil and encourages viewers to reconsider their preconceptions about individuals with mental disorders.

Overcoming Fear

The theme of overcoming fear is interwoven throughout the film. The characters' struggles against Kevin and their eventual attempts to escape signify a broader battle against fear itself. Shyamalan urges the audience to confront their worst fears rather than run from them, suggesting that true strength comes from facing challenges head-on. This theme resonates with many viewers, as it is a universal aspect of the human experience.

Conclusion: A Multifaceted Narrative

Split is more than just a thriller; it’s a deep exploration of identity, trauma, and the complexities of human nature. M. Night Shyamalan masterfully weaves together these themes to create a narrative that not only entertains but also encourages audiences to reflect on their own understanding of self and others. The film leaves viewers questioning the nature of identity and empathy in a world that often stigmatizes mental health issues.
