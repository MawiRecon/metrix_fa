What’s the deal?: Kamala Harris

Published June 2025

Kamala Harris has made history as the first woman, the first Black woman, and the first person of South Asian descent to serve as Vice President of the United States. Her ascent to this position reflects both her personal resolve and the broader societal changes in the United States, as she continues to break barriers and inspire future generations of leaders.

Kamala Harris: Her Journey, Impact, and Controversies

Kamala Harris: Her Journey, Impact, and Controversies

Early Life and Education

Kamala Devi Harris was born on October 20, 1964, in Oakland, California. She attended Howard University, a historically Black university, where she earned her Bachelor of Arts degree in political science and economics. After that, she attended the University of California, Hastings College of the Law, earning her Juris Doctor in 1989. Her educational background laid a solid foundation for her future political career, highlighting her advocacy for social justice and equality.

Political Career

Harris's political career began in the Alameda County District Attorney's Office in California, where she gained a reputation for her tough stance on crime, particularly regarding domestic violence. In 2003, she was elected as the District Attorney of San Francisco, making her the first woman and first Black person to hold that office.

In 2010, she was elected Attorney General of California, where she focused on issues such as housing, healthcare, and the environment. Her work in this role further built her profile as a leader willing to tackle complex problems. In 2016, Harris was elected to the U.S. Senate, where she established herself as a fierce advocate for civil rights, immigration reform, and criminal justice reform.

Vice Presidency

In 2020, Kamala Harris was selected by Joe Biden as his running mate in the presidential election. Their campaign focused on unity, recovery from the COVID-19 pandemic, and addressing systemic racism. After winning the election, Harris was sworn in as Vice President on January 20, 2021, marking a significant milestone in U.S. history.

Controversies and Challenges

Throughout her career, Harris has attracted both praise and criticism. One of the most debated topics surrounds her tenure as Attorney General, where some activists argue that her policies disproportionately affected communities of color, particularly in the realm of criminal justice. Critics have pointed to her past decisions on prosecuting non-violent drug offenders as evidence that she may not be as progressive as she claims. This has led to a divided opinion among her supporters and detractors.

Controversial Opinion: Balancing Justice and Equity

While many view Kamala Harris as a champion for progressive policies, one controversial opinion is that her past as a prosecutor undermines her credibility with some of the very communities she aims to support. Critics argue that her decisions in office reflect a more traditional, "tough on crime" approach, which may not align with the progressive vision for reform that many expect from her. This complexity raises the question: Can she effectively advocate for systemic change while having a history rooted in the more traditional legal frameworks that many activists oppose?

Legacy and Future Outlook

Kamala Harris's legacy is still being formed as she navigates the complexities of her role as Vice President. Her influence on policy, particularly regarding healthcare, immigration, and civil rights, will likely define her time in office. As the U.S. continues to confront deep-seated issues, her ability to address these challenges will be vital, and the nation will be watching closely.

Conclusion

Kamala Harris stands as a symbol of change within American politics, reflecting the diverse fabric of the nation. Her journey from a young girl in Oakland to the Vice Presidency is inspiring, yet her path is not without its challenges and controversies. As we consider the implications of her leadership, it is crucial to engage in discussions about both her achievements and the complexities of her legacy.
