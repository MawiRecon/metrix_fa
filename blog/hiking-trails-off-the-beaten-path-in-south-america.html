What’s the Deal?: Hiking Trails Off the Beaten Path in South America

Published June 2025

South America is a treasure trove of unparalleled natural beauty, boasting diverse landscapes from towering mountains to lush rainforests. While popular destinations like Machu Picchu or Torres del Paine often steal the limelight, there are equally mesmerizing hiking trails that remain undiscovered by the mainstream traveler. In this post, we will explore some of the most breathtaking hiking trails off the beaten path in South America.

Hiking Trails Off the Beaten Path in South America

1. The Huayhuash Circuit, Peru

The Huayhuash Circuit is a stunning alternative to the more crowded Inca Trail. Nestled in the Andes mountains, it spans approximately 130 kilometers (about 81 miles) and takes about 10 to 12 days to complete. The trail boasts breathtaking vistas of glacial lakes, towering peaks, and unique Andean wildlife.

What makes this trail special is its remoteness; very few trekkers venture here, giving you the opportunity to experience tranquility amidst nature's grandeur. Along the way, you will encounter small indigenous villages that add a cultural richness to your adventure.

2. The Lost City Trek, Colombia

Located deep in the Colombian rainforest, the Lost City (Ciudad Perdida) Trek is a thrilling journey that takes you through unspoiled territories inhabited by diverse wildlife. The trek is about 44 kilometers (27 miles) long and usually takes about 4 to 6 days to complete.

During your hike, expect to cross rivers, navigate steep trails, and pass through dense jungle before reaching the ancient ruins of the Lost City, which were built by the Tairona civilization. This unique blend of stunning natural beauty and rich history makes the Lost City Trek an unforgettable experience.

3. The Puyuhuapi Traverse, Chile

For those who want to experience the breathtaking contrasts of Patagonia, the Puyuhuapi Traverse is a significant yet lesser-known trek. This trail leads you from the lush green valleys of the Chilean fjords to the breathtaking peaks of the Andes. Stretching over 70 kilometers (43 miles), it typically takes about 6 days to finish.

The trek includes stunning viewpoints, cascading waterfalls, and pristine lakes, all while passing through ancient woodlands. The Puyuhuapi Traverse is truly a hidden gem for hikers seeking a mix of wilderness and adventure.

4. The Salkantay Trek, Peru

Often overshadowed by the Inca Trail, the Salkantay Trek is a breathtaking trail that offers stunning views of the Salkantay Mountain. With diverse ecosystems and cultural encounters, this trek takes you through scenic valleys, enchanting cloud forests, and ancient ruins, covering approximately 70 kilometers (43 miles) over 5 days.

This less-crowded alternative starts from the town of Mollepata and ends in Aguas Calientes, the gateway to Machu Picchu. Trekkers can enjoy awe-inspiring landscapes and a chance to soak in natural hot springs along the way.

5. The Chacabuco Valley, Chile

A lesser-known trail in Patagonia, the Chacabuco Valley offers impressive views of the Andes without the heavy foot traffic. It provides access to beautiful landscapes characterized by rivers, forests, and a variety of wildlife. This area is less frequented, giving you a chance to experience untouched nature.

The hike can be tailored to various levels of fitness and experience, ranging from short day hikes to multi-day backcountry explorations, making it ideal for all outdoor enthusiasts.

Conclusion

These off-the-beaten-path trails in South America are just a glimpse into the countless opportunities for adventure that this continent has to offer. Whether you are seeking stunning vistas, rich history, or pristine nature, you’ll find it in these lesser-known hiking destinations. A journey on these trails not only promises breathtaking experiences but also helps you create memories that will last a lifetime.
