What’s the Deal?: Hyperpop

Published June 2025

In the ever-evolving landscape of music genres, Hyperpop has emerged as a dazzling blend of experimentation, digital sounds, and unabashed creativity. Characterized by its upbeat tempos, pitch-shifted vocals, and a fusion of various influences including pop, hip-hop, and electronic, Hyperpop transcends traditional genre boundaries, setting the stage for a musical revolution.

Hyperpop: The Genre That's Redefining Music

What is Hyperpop?

Hyperpop is a genre that first gained traction in the late 2010s through platforms like SoundCloud and TikTok. It encapsulates a wide array of musical styles, often incorporating elements from bubblegum pop, EDM, and even punk rock. One of the defining features of Hyperpop is its playful and exaggerated aesthetic, both sonically and visually. Artists often leverage digital production techniques to create a sound that is at once futuristic and entirely new.

The Origins of Hyperpop

The roots of Hyperpop can be traced back to the Internet's influence on music consumption and production. Key figures like A.G. Cook of PC Music, along with other collaborators from the Internet's underground music scene, began to experiment with the conventions of pop to create something more vibrant and diverse. The genre gained further popularity with the release of tracks by artists such as Charli XCX and 100 gecs, who have played critical roles in shaping its sound and aesthetic.

Key Characteristics of Hyperpop

  • Euphoric Energy: Hyperpop tracks typically have a driving beat and catchy hooks that create a euphoric listening experience.
  • Layered Production: Complex production techniques, featuring pitch-shifted vocals, synth-heavy instrumentals, and heavy use of Auto-Tune.
  • Visual Aesthetics: Artists often embrace a hyper-stylized, colorful aesthetic that reflects the exuberance of their music.
  • Genre Blending: Hyperpop is not confined to one sound, incorporating elements from various genres to create a unique sonic palette.

Cultural Impact of Hyperpop

The impact of Hyperpop extends beyond music; it has permeated fashion, art, and digital culture. The genre's playful use of social media platforms for marketing has allowed many underground artists to reach global audiences. Hyperpop serves as a voice for a younger generation, often tackling themes such as identity, technology, and queerness in ways that resonate deeply with listeners.

Future of Hyperpop

As the music industry continues to evolve, Hyperpop seems poised to have a lasting impact. With the rise of new artists and ongoing experimentation, the genre is likely to expand and influence future sounds in unexpected ways. It challenges the normative definitions of what pop music can encompass, paving the way for even more diverse sounds and messages to emerge.

In conclusion, Hyperpop is more than just a genre; it is a cultural phenomenon that embodies the spirit of experimentation and innovation in music today. As it continues to grow, so too will its influence on both the music industry and broader culture.
