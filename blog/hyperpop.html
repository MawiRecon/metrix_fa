What’s the deal?: Hyperpop

Published June 2025

Hyperpop is an exciting and rapidly emerging genre that has captured the hearts of music enthusiasts around the globe. Bursting onto the scene in the late 2010s, Hyperpop is characterized by its exaggerated use of pop elements, vibrant aesthetics, and genre-bending productions. This blog post aims to dive deep into Hyperpop, exploring its origins, key artists, its unique sound, and the debates surrounding its artistic value.

Understanding Hyperpop: The New Musical Revolution

Understanding Hyperpop: The New Musical Revolution

What is Hyperpop?

At its core, Hyperpop is a fusion of various genres including pop, electronic, hip-hop, and avant-garde music. It embodies a playful approach to production, often featuring high-pitched vocals, glitchy sound effects, and upbeat tempos. The use of Auto-Tune and pitch correction is prevalent, creating an almost surreal and otherworldly sound. This genre defies traditional structures and embraces chaos while making it appealing to a broad audience.

The Origins of Hyperpop

The term "Hyperpop" was popularized by the collective PC Music, co-founded by A.G. Cook, in the mid-2010s. This collective's influence helped shape the genre's identity, with its emphasis on the hyperreality of contemporary culture. Artists like Charlie XCX, 100 gecs, and 070 Shake have each contributed to the genre's evolution, incorporating their unique styles while staying true to the Hyperpop ethos.

Key Players in the Hyperpop Scene

Many artists have made significant impacts within the Hyperpop genre. Some of the most notable include:

  • Charlie XCX: Known for her experimental approach and mainstream success, she has become a leading figure in Hyperpop.
  • 100 gecs: The duo’s groundbreaking sound and viral hits exemplify the genre's chaotic style.
  • A.G. Cook: A producer and the mastermind behind PC Music, Cook's work has been pivotal in shaping Hyperpop.
  • Gupi: One of the more experimental artists, Gupi’s music blends various influences and attracts a diverse fan base.
  • Arca: While not exclusively Hyperpop, Arca's innovative sound and visual aesthetics align closely with the genre’s avant-garde nature.

The Aesthetic of Hyperpop

Hyperpop is not just a musical genre; it is an aesthetic movement. The visuals accompanying Hyperpop music often feature bright colors, surreal graphics, and a fusion of high and low culture. This combination creates a visual representation of the sonic chaos inherent in the music. Artists use social media platforms, particularly TikTok and Instagram, to showcase their hyper-stylized identities, making the genre as much about community as it is about sound.

Hyperpop's Controversial Aspect

While Hyperpop has garnered a dedicated following, some critics argue that it lacks depth and authenticity when compared to more traditional music genres. These critics assert that the genre’s reliance on production techniques and digital manipulation undermines the emotional connection typically associated with music. This perspective raises an important discussion about the definition of artistry in the age of technology. Can something so heavily produced still be considered 'real' music? While many celebrate Hyperpop for its innovation, others believe it may lead to a future where music becomes merely a product of algorithm-driven trends rather than a genuine expression of human emotion.

The Future of Hyperpop

As with any evolving genre, the future of Hyperpop remains uncertain but filled with potential. With its growing popularity, there is the possibility of further mainstream infiltration, which could lead to a broader acceptance and understanding of its unique characteristics. As new artists emerge and experimental soundscapes continue to develop, Hyperpop is poised to reshape our musical landscape in exciting ways.

Conclusion

Hyperpop is a genre that challenges conventional boundaries and embraces the eclectic nature of modern music. It embodies a unique cultural movement that reflects our rapidly changing digital world. As we continue to see its evolution, it is clear that Hyperpop is more than just a fleeting trend; it is a cutting-edge form of artistic expression that deserves our attention and appreciation.
