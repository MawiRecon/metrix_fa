What’s the Deal?: A History of Formula 1 (F1)

Published June 2025

Discover how Formula 1 evolved from its early days to become one of the most popular motorsport events in the world.

A History of Formula 1 (F1)

The Birth of Formula 1

Formula 1, often abbreviated as F1, originated in Europe after World War II. The inaugural World Championship of Drivers took place in 1950, featuring a series of races known as Grands Prix. Initially, the series was dominated by Italian teams and drivers. Alfa Romeo secured a remarkable victory, with Giuseppe Farina being crowned the first-ever F1 World Champion.

Technological Advancements and Innovations

Throughout the decades, F1 has been synonymous with technological innovation. The 1960s and 1970s ushered in a wave of advancements, with teams like Ferrari and Lotus pioneering innovations such as aerodynamics and suspension systems. The introduction of the ground effect in the late 1970s allowed cars to generate additional downforce, fundamentally changing the design and performance of F1 cars.

The Era of Legends

The history of F1 is marked by legendary drivers such as Ayrton Senna, Alain Prost, and Michael Schumacher. Senna and Prost's intense rivalry defined the late 1980s and early 1990s, culminating in thrilling races filled with controversial moments. Michael Schumacher’s reign in the late 1990s and early 2000s set new records, including seven world championships, a feat that stood until it was matched by Lewis Hamilton in recent years.

Safety Measures and Regulations

As F1 progressed, so did the focus on safety. The tragic deaths of prominent figures such as Ayrton Senna in 1994 triggered significant changes in safety regulations. The FIA (Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile) implemented measures such as crash tests, improved car design, and stronger helmet standards, leading to a striking decrease in fatalities over the years. Today's F1 circuits feature advanced safety barriers and improved medical facilities.

Modern Era and Global Expansion

In the 21st century, Formula 1 has experienced massive growth, with an ever-expanding global fanbase. The advent of social media has allowed fans to engage with the sport in unprecedented ways. Liberty Media's acquisition of F1 in 2017 brought a fresh perspective, increasing visibility while expanding the sport's reach to audiences worldwide. Major changes, such as the introduction of hybrid engines in 2014, have also fueled interest by highlighting a commitment to sustainability and innovation in motorsport.
