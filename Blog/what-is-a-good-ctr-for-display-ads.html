What Is a Good Click-Through Rate (CTR) for Display Ads?

Published June 2025

If you’ve ever launched a display campaign, you’ve probably asked the question: “Is my CTR good?”

CTR (click-through rate) is the ratio of users who click your ad compared to the number of impressions. For display ads, the average CTR typically ranges from 0.1% to 0.5%.

Why It’s Usually Low

Unlike search ads where users are actively looking for something, display ads are passive. They're shown to people browsing content, not necessarily ready to act. That’s why CTRs are naturally lower.

What’s Considered “Good”?

  • Above 0.3%: Solid for most industries
  • Above 0.5%: Strong performance
  • 1% or higher: Exceptional (often retargeting)

However, don’t obsess over CTR alone. It’s just one metric. Sometimes, a lower CTR with better conversions is more valuable than a high CTR with no sales.

Keep testing creative, refining audiences, and checking post-click behavior. That’s how you truly optimize performance — not just chase a higher CTR.
